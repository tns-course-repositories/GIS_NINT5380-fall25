Final Project Overview

The final project is a core component of this course; it serves as a significant evaluation tool ensuring mastery of course material and technical aspects of the course. It also allows for exploration of course concepts and mastered skills for a specific research topic of your own choosing. As noted in the Evaluations and Grading section of the syllabus, the final project is worth 35% of the total grade - 10% is given to the stages leading up to the final presentation (starting now); and 25% is given to the completed final project organization and upload. The grading weights are excerpted from the syllabus as follows:

Evaluations and Grading:

Course Component Percentage
Weekly GIS technical assignments 50%
Independent research, data development & project scope (Final Project) 10%
Final GIS project presentation (Final Project) 25%
Participation & weekly Quizzes 15%
Total 100%

Three Stage Process

The final project will be developed across 3 stages.

The first stage - this assignment - is the asking of a GIS question.

Once a GIS question is proposed and then refined, the second stage will leverage the refined GIS question into a full project proposal inclusive of data acquisition and selected spatial analysis methodology.

The third stage will be the analysis itself and the development of project findings/outcomes, and reporting out those findings/outcomes in the form of a presentation and a short methods paper.

For this stage I assignment, there is no need to deliberate the second and third stages; rather, focus more narrowly on the GIS question itself. In response to the assignment deliverable, instructor will give feedback and suggest refinements and ‚Äònext steps‚Äô in preparation of the second stage.
