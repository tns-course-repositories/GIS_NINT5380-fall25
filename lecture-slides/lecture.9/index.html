Spatial Statistics I

Data Normalization

The purpose of normalization is to transform data in a way that they are either dimensionless and/or have similar distributions. This process of normalization is known by other names such as standardization, feature scaling etc. Normalization is an essential step in data pre-processing in any machine learning application and model fitting.

Source:Data Normalization

Spatial Statistics I

Data Preparation: Data Normalization - Technique I

Rescaling - Multiplying each member of a data set by a constant term k. The range of the data changes.

Source:Data Normalization

Spatial Statistics I

Data Preparation: Data Normalization - Technique II

Normalizing - Values set to common scale, without distorting differences in range of values. Shape of the distribution of the data changes.

Source:Data Normalization

Spatial Statistics I

Data Preparation: Data Normalization - Technique III

Standardizing - Z-score standardization is a procedure to “standardize” data to have mean = 0 and standard deviation = 1.

Source:Data Standardizing

Spatial Statistics II

Spatial statistics differ from traditional statistics in that spatial statistics integrate space and spatial relationships in the analysis. Spatial statistics can be used with continuous data as well as three different types of object data: points, lines, and areas (Bailey and Gatrell 1995).

Source:Spatial Statistics Short Definition

Spatial Statistics II

Spatial Autocorrelation

Most statistical methods are based on certain assumptions such as that the samples are independent of each other. Geographic phenomena, however, are all related to each other as Waldo R. Tobler’s First Law of Geography states: “Everything is related to everything else, but near things are more related than distant things.”

Source:First Law of Geography
